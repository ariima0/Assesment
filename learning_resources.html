Rules Governing the Use of the Library and Resource Centre

All students and staff of the College are entitled to register as members of the Library and Resource Centre and use the facilities for the purpose of their course of study or College employment.

Membership may also be extended to other individuals and persons nominated from other organisations for a fee. Access may be made available to non-members by arrangement. Details are available in the External Services leaflet.

Use of facilities implies acceptance of the Library and Resource Centre rules printed below. These rules are designed to protect all users' interests and ensure the efficient operation of the College within the law. The rules apply to all sites, including workstation rooms and site libraries, with some variations.

Rules

  1. Access:
    • The use of Library and Resource Centre facilities is limited to educational and College-related purposes.
    • All individuals wishing to become members must comply with registration procedures, including an agreement to abide by Library rules.
    • Non-eligible individuals or organisations may be admitted by arrangement for study purposes only. Procedures are outlined in the External Services leaflet. Non-members must sign the Visitors' Book and provide written ID.
    • ID cards are non-transferable. The person named on each card is responsible for borrowed items and their replacement in case of loss or damage.
    • Users must be willing to show ID cards or other identification upon request.
  2. Use of Facilities:
    • The Library is not responsible for the loss or damage to personal belongings. Users must take reasonable precautions and avoid leaving belongings unattended.
    • Users must maintain a quiet atmosphere, with minimal conversation. No conversation is permitted in designated 'Silent' areas.
    • Personal stereos may not be used in the Library.
    • Mobile phones must be turned off in study areas. Use is allowed in the enclosed stairwells of the Main Site building.
    • Users should not connect personal equipment to mains electrical supplies unless it meets Health & Safety standards. Written proof of compliance is required.
    • Eating and drinking are not permitted in any public areas.
    • Misuse of furniture, facilities, or materials may result in disciplinary action and charges for damages.
